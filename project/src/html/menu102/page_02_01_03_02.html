To achieve victory in a race, selecting the right strategy and utilizing technical skills proficiently are crucial.

Strategy refers to various racing methods used to win, and it is one of the key factors that determine the outcome.
Track cycling athletes choose the appropriate strategy that matches their condition and the race situation, showcasing their best performance.

Lead Strategy

A racing method where the cyclist leads the race from the front before passing the first corner of the final lap.

Quick-timed Overtake Strategy

The quick-timed overtake strategy in cycling is a tactic used to dramatically overtake other cyclists in the final stage of the race.
Even if a cyclist is behind until just before the last lap, they can still seize victory through this strategy. This makes a quick-timed overtake one of the most thrilling aspects of cycling races.

Late Charge Strategy

In cycling, the late charge strategy involves a cyclist staying behind the leading competitors to avoid wind resistance and conserve energy. Then, in the final phase of the race, the cyclist follows the leaders
and attempts a dramatic overtake at the last moment. This strategy requires patience and explosive speed and is one of the more demanding strategies.

Marking Strategy

In the marking strategy, two or more cyclists form a team and cooperate with one another during the race.
The marker cyclist protects the lead cyclist and creates opportunities for them, while the lead cyclist relies on the marker's assistance to guide the race in their favour.
This strategy is reliant on teamwork and strategies.
